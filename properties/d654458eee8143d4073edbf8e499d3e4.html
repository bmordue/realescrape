Flat

2 Bedrooms

This two-bedroom flat offers a quiet central location, situated along a no-through road leading directly to Holyrood Park, with serene views enjoyed from inside. It affords all the light and space expected of a tenement home, with classical proportions and well-presented décor in a subtle colour palette. Adding further appeal is an attractive shared garden, and permit parking is available on the street. A good range of amenities and attractions are close by, and Waverley train station, along with the heart of the city, is easily accessible by foot. The flat lies on the first floor reached via a communal stairwell with a secure entry system. Welcoming you inside is a roomy entrance hall offset by white walls and honey-coloured flooring. The hall (with practical built-in storage) leads into the generous reception room. The comfortably carpeted area promises a flexible space for lounge seating and dining furniture, neutrally decorated and filled with natural light from a large window capturing park views. Elegant cornicework and a central rose adorn the room, which is completed by handy recessed shelving. Adjacent to the reception area is a contemporary kitchen, designed in earthy tones with oak-inspired units, an illuminated countertop, and a mosaic-patterned floor. It comes equipped with freestanding appliances, comprising an electric cooker, a washing machine, and a tall fridge freezer. Quietly located to the rear of the property are two spacious bedrooms, with the largest room featuring built-in cupboard storage. Both rooms exude a relaxing ambience with delicate pastel décor and soft carpeting, complemented by a pleasant garden outlook. Finally, a bright bathroom is simply styled in all white, with a WC suite, a bath with an overhead shower, and a tiled surround heightened by decorative accents. The property benefits from modern electric thermal heating and full double-glazing. Externally, residents of the tenement share access to a large rear garden with a neatly tended lawn. On-street parking along Waverley Park falls under Zone N6. Abbeyhill Edinburgh Situated close to Edinburgh's iconic Royal Mile, Abbeyhill offers easy access to the world-class entertainment and shopping found in the heart of the capital. The area is currently enjoying a surge in popularity thanks to the wealth of independent shops and vibrant restaurants, pubs, and cafés in the immediate area. It also benefits from being close to the thriving amenities of Leith Walk and Easter Road, as well as Meadowbank Retail Park, which boasts a Sainsbury's and a Lidl, plus several additional high-street stores and fast-food outlets. Furthermore, the St James Quarter is within easy reach, the £1billion development hosting world-class designer stores, trendy bars, entertainment facilities, and a diverse range of restaurants and eateries. Sports enthusiasts are well-catered for by nearby Meadowbank Sports Centre, which provides everything from multi-purpose sports halls and athletic tracks to fitness classes and a state-of-the-art gym. Those who prefer to exercise in the great outdoors can opt for a walk up Salisbury Crags and Arthur's Seat, or a slightly more leisurely climb up Calton Hill, all affording panoramic views across the city. There is also excellent public and private schooling nearby, from nursery level upwards. The area is extremely well-connected with regular day and night bus services and good access to commuter routes, while Waverley train station is within walking distance.
