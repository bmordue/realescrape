Eilean Mo Chrihe, Brodick, Isle of Arran, North
Ayrshire KA27 8DP

Offers over £210,000

Detached Bungalow

2 Bedrooms

Eliean Mo Chrihe is a quaint two-bedroom bungalow located within the general urban area of the village of Brodick. The property, constructed in the distinctive 'Dorran' style, features a single-storey layout with prefabricated single skin concrete walls and no insulation. While finance may not be available for this type of property, the bungalow presents a unique opportunity for accommodation prior to future redevelopment. Set within an expansive plot, the property boasts impressive front and rear gardens, ample off-road parking, and a peaceful location on the banks of the Glen Rosa burn. The property can be accessed via a bright vestibule entrance at the front of the property, providing a practical space for coats and shoes. Inside, the spacious open-plan living area is simple yet inviting. The living space is designed to offer a comfortable and versatile layout, making it ideal for hosting and socialising. The connection of the living room, kitchen, and dining areas creates a seamless flow within the bungalow. The kitchen features a well-organised layout with clean, white shaker style units, complemented by a wood-effect countertop. An extension of the countertop forms a convenient breakfast bar, ideal for casual dining or additional preparation space. At the end of the kitchen, sliding doors flood the room with light and provide easy access to the garden. The adjoining dining area overlooks the garden and burn, offering a peaceful view while enjoying meals with family or guests. Connected to the kitchen, a generously sized utility room offers ample space for white goods and provides seamless access to the back garden, serving as a secondary vestibule to the home. The two bedrooms are generously sized with the second bedroom benefiting from direct access to a dressing room, offering extra storage and functional space for clothing. The bathroom, accessible from the dressing room, is a three-piece suite with a clean, functional design. It's a private sanctuary, perfect for unwinding. The shower room, located at the end of the hall features a toilet, sink, and well appointed shower which is enveloped by quadrant shower doors. Eliean Mo Chrihe offers a rare opportunity for buyers with a vision, as the existing bungalow will be replaced with a stunning new 4-bedroom, storey-and-a-half detached home. Approved under Outline Planning this new dwelling will be a high-end, executive property set in one of Arran's most prestigious locations - on the banks of the picturesque Glen Rosa burn. The proposed design blends seamlessly with the natural beauty of the surroundings, with the south-west elevation featuring a charming rural cottage style, while the north-east elevation opens up to expansive glazing and a raised balcony, maximising the breathtaking views over the garden and burn. The scale and style of the new home has been carefully designed to complement the adjacent properties, including Greystones and the recently built Catriona, ensuring a harmonious fit within this sought-after area. With generous garden space and a driveway, this is an exceptional opportunity to build a spectacular, bespoke home in a truly special setting, just a short walk from Brodick village centre and with the golf club on your doorstep. End Value (Once Constructed) £625,000/£650,000 Area Eilean Mo Chrihe ' is located in a spectacular position on the outskirts of Brodick on the beautiful Isle of Arran. The Isle of Arran (aka 'Scotland in Miniature') has everything to offer - a welcoming community, forest trails and mountain peaks, sheltered beaches, and a vast range of local heritage, crafts and produce. Brodick, Arran's capital, has two Co-op supermarkets, a great selection of individual shops, restaurants, cafes and a full range of services including a primary school, nursery, library and medical centre. Brodick hosts the island's main ferry terminal from which there are regular sailings throughout the day, serviced by Caledonian MacBrayne, to the mainland at Ardrossan. Commuters and travellers now benefit from Road Equivalent Tariff (ret) ferry fares. The port of Ardrossan has a mainline rail station, with direct trains to Glasgow that connect with the ferry times, and is approximately 30 miles from Glasgow City Centre.
