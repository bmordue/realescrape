Morven, Abernyte,
Perth PH149ST

Offers Over £160,000

Detached House

3 Bedrooms

Lindsays are delighted to present Morven, a traditional cottage set within the charming and sought after village of Abernyte, offering outstanding potential and a beautiful rural outlook. The property, which would benefit from a degree of modernisation, provides a rare opportunity for buyers wishing to place their own stamp on a well-loved home in a peaceful countryside setting.

On arrival, the property is approached via a generous driveway that provides ample parking for multiple vehicles and includes an accessible ramp leading to the entrance. The porch opens into a welcoming hallway that connects to the main living accommodation. The living room is bright and spacious, enjoying an attractive open view across the Carse of Gowrie, while the adjacent kitchen sits conveniently off the living area. The ground floor also features a large double bedroom offering comfortable proportions, along with a modernised wetroom style shower room. Upstairs, the cottage benefits from two well-sized attic rooms, each offering excellent versatility and suitable for a range of uses.

Externally, the property is complemented by useful outbuildings that present a variety of potential uses or possible future development opportunities, subject to the necessary consents. The front garden is laid to lawn and enjoys lovely open views, enhancing the sense of tranquillity. In addition, a larger garden plot is located directly opposite the cottage, providing further outdoor space ideal for gardening, recreation, or future landscaping ideas.

Overall, Morven represents an excellent opportunity to acquire a traditional home in a scenic rural location, with substantial scope to refurbish, enhance and adapt the property to suit individual needs.


Area

Abernyte is a peaceful rural village set within the scenic Carse of Gowrie, offering rolling countryside and a friendly community atmosphere. Located between Dundee and Perth, it benefits from convenient transport links while retaining its tranquil character. Local features include a primary school, community groups and the nearby Scottish Antique & Arts Centre, with the Sidlaw Hills providing excellent outdoor recreation opportunities.
