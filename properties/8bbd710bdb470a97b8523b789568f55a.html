Area - Innerleithen, Scottish BordersLying close to the banks of the River Tweed and nestled amongst rolling hills, the former mill and spa town of Innerleithen enjoys an idyllic Scottish Borders setting. The town is served by a local supermarket, a library contact centre, a health centre, a pharmacy, a post office and several independent shops, including bakeries and a butcher. A selection of eateries is also on offer, whilst further amenities can be found a short drive away in the charming market town of Peebles. The town's scenic surroundings provide endless opportunities for outdoor pursuits such as fishing, walking and cycling, while nearby Glentress Forest Park boasts a treetop adventure course. Innerleithen also hosts its own park and a local history museum at St Ronan's Wells. Primary and nursery schooling is provided locally at St Ronan's Primary School, followed by secondary education at Peebles High School. Commuting to the capital takes approximately an hour by road, whilst Galashiels - located 20 minutes' drive from Innerleithen - provides fast and frequent rail connections as part of the Borders Railway.
