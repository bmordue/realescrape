Leith is a vibrant and diverse district located just to the north of Edinburgh's city centre, with a rich history as the capital's historic port. Over the years, Leith has evolved into one of the city's most dynamic and sought-after areas, known for its eclectic blend of traditional charm and modern development. Historically a bustling maritime hub, Leith's waterfront along the Shore is still a key feature, with its cobbled streets, historic warehouses, and picturesque views of the docks. The area is now home to an array of trendy bars, restaurants, cafes, and galleries, offering a lively atmosphere that attracts both locals and visitors alike. The district has a strong cultural identity, with a thriving arts scene and regular events that celebrate its heritage and
contemporary creative spirit.

Leith also boasts a mix of architecture, from traditional tenements and historic buildings to modern apartments and converted warehouses, providing a variety of housing options. Its sense of community is reinforced by independent shops, local markets, and a growing number of businesses that reflect the area's modern, entrepreneurial energy. In addition to its vibrant social scene, Leith offers a selection of green spaces, including Leith Links and the nearby Water of Leith Walkway, perfect for outdoor activities. It is also well-served by public transport, making it easy to travel into the city centre or beyond. Overall, Leith is an area that celebrates its maritime heritage while embracing modernity, making it one of Edinburgh's most exciting and evolving neighbourhoods.
