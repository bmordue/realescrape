Alexander House, 5 Alexander Street, Dysart,
Kirkcaldy KY1 2XX

Offers over £499,500

Detached House

5 Bedrooms

Outstanding 5 Bed Detached Executive Villa: Welcome to Alexander House, an expansive family home, designed and reimagined with light and space in mind, occupying a peaceful position on a quiet road in the historic village of Dysart. A sympathetic renovation under current ownership has made expert use of space, while retaining charm, comfort and practicality.

The internal space offers immense living and bedroom accommodation, with flowing, flexible and light-filled spaces, contemporary dining kitchen and breakfasting rooms, multiple living areas, modern, 4 pristine, unique bathrooms and 5+ generous bedrooms. A perfect home for the growing family and those who want to live within lush, expressive spaces. The property also benefits from two utility areas and a large store-room / home office. Outside, you will be impressed with the private, enclosed and sunny courtyard garden to the front which is an ideal spot for alfresco dining and entertaining friends and family. As would be expected, there is lots of parking space in addition. You will also find a summer house and external store-room in this area, both with power and light and to the rear of the house there is a secluded, grassed garden area. This property also benfits from solar panels.

This property is very well located, with excellent local amenities in the picturesque and historic village of Dysart. Dysart boasts a selection of shopping and local facilities and there is a nearby nursery and primary school, museum and a community centre as well as the lovely foreshore and the restored harbour which gained more recent fame by featuring in the television series 'Outlander'. Ravenscraig Park is nearby and a short drive to Kirkcaldy will take you to more excellent amenities including a leisure centre, library, museum, cafes and restaurants, Beveridge Park and numerous schools and educational facilities, as well as a good selection of shops and retail parks. The railway station is a short drive off, providing rail links to Edinburgh and Dundee and Kirkcaldy also benefits from its own bus station. There is very easy access to the motorway networks leading to Dundee, Edinburgh, and beyond.
