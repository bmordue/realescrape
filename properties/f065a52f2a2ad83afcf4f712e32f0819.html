10 Bank Park Grange is a modern detached brick-built 5-bedroom house located in the market town of Tranent in East Lothian.

This property offers stunning elevated open views to the East Lothian coastline, providing a picturesque backdrop.

As you enter through the vestibule, you are greeted by a spacious hallway with hardwood flooring that's runs throughout the ground floor. The property is well presented and boasts a bright and airy atmosphere throughout.

The kitchen is spacious and well-appointed, offering ample storage and workspace. It is seamlessly connected to a large dining room, providing a perfect setting for hosting family gatherings or entertaining guests. Additionally, there is a separate utility room, offering convenience and practicality.

The sitting room features a charming feature fireplace, adding character and warmth to the room along with French doors to a patio area.

The ground floor also includes a convenient WC and a versatile fifth bedroom, which can easily double as a study or home office, catering to your individual needs.

Upstairs, you will find four well-proportioned bedrooms, providing plenty of space for the whole family. The principal bedroom benefits from an en-suite shower room, ensuring privacy and comfort. There is also a separate family bathroom, offering convenience and serving the other bedrooms.

The property boasts a driveway with a detached double garage, providing secure parking and additional storage space. There is also a separate parking space available at the front of the house.

The terraced gardens of 10 Bank Park Grange are a true highlight, featuring a spacious lawned area with views towards the coast and a raised patio.

In summary, this modern detached house offers a comfortable and stylish living environment. With its elevated position and stunning views of the East Lothian coastline, spacious rooms, detached double garage, and delightful gardens.

Area

The Historic town of Tranent in East Lothian is located just off the A1 offering a mix of modern and traditional housing developments.

The town itself offers a diverse range of good food and vibrant specialist shops. A doctor's surgery, leisure centre, library, and post office including Asda and Aldi supermarket.

Surrounded by beautiful countryside, Tranent is well- placed for access to the East Lothian coastline and many superb beaches.

Fort Kinnaird and Straiton retail parks have an extensive range of major High-Street names, restaurants and a multi-screen cinema.

There are numerous golf courses close-by including the Championship courses of Muirfield, North Berwick and Dunbar to name just a few.

Superb white sandy beaches run along the coastline are easily reached by car or public transport.

Tranent has many leisure facilities including a swimming pool, and close-by Meadowmill Sports and fitness centre and an indoor bowling club.

Commuting into Edinburgh, there is direct access via the A1 to the city bypass and A199. There are also regular bus services available from the High Street, and train services from nearby Musselburgh, Wallyford, or Prestonpans.
