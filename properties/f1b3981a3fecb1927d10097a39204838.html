37/2 Buckingham Terrace,
Edinburgh EH4 3AP

Offers over £475,000

First Floor Flat

2 Bedrooms

Located in the highly sought after West End of Edinburgh in a quiet cobbled street is this striking B-Listed Drawing Room Flat. Occupying the whole of the first floor of this beautiful Victorian terraced villa, the property offers exceptionally spacious and bright accommodation perfectly blending contemporary interiors and original Victorian charm and character. The property is within strolling distance to Belgrave Crescent Gardens (on application and payment of a small annual fee).

With gas central heating there is a spacious hallway with cupboard and larger utility cupboard. There is a lovely lounge with window to front, wood flooring, door to hallway and bi-folding doors lead to the living/dining kitchen and it is thought that this room could be utilised as a third double bedroom is desired. The main focal point of this fantastic flat is the large living / dining / kitchen with its large bay window to front, ceiling cornicing and wood flooring. The kitchen is fitted with white gloss base and wall units with integrated oven, hob, hood, fridge and dishwasher. There is a large double bedroom with double wardrobe and deep storage cupboard and a further double bedroom, both of which overlook the rear of the property. Completing the accommodation is the bathroom with three piece white suite with shower over the bath and window to rear.

Early internal viewing is essential to fully appreciate the large rooms, beautiful original features and the fantastic location.

Buckingham Terrace is located in the heart of Edinburgh's West End and is conveniently located close to Princes Street, George Street and Stockbridge. The property has access to Belgrave Crescent Gardens which are a private, Victorian garden in Edinburgh's World Heritage Site.

A hotspot of culture, the West End boasts an impressive number of theatres, festivals and craft fairs. Stylish boutiques combine with popular pubs and bars to give the whole area a bohemian feel; local residents often refer to the area as The Village. With an impressive network of wonderful, Georgian properties, and quaint, cobbled streets, you can spend your weekends wandering around the quirky shops and restaurants. Just a few minutes' walk from Princes Street, and the bustling centre of Edinburgh, the West End is also a stone's throw from Haymarket Railway Station, which offers excellent transport links across the whole of the United Kingdom.
