LOCATION
Fearnan is a charming village nestled on the northern shore of Loch Tay. It is celebrated for its breathtaking scenery, including stunning views of the surrounding hills and Loch Tay itself. The village is a blend of old crofts and more modern buildings. The remnants of a hidden village of crofts can be found below the forestry of Drummond Hill. 

Nearby Aberfeldy, is the geographical heart of Scotland situated on Scotland’s longest river, the River Tay and is home to the Birks Cinema and Dewar’s Distillery. You will also find a good selection of local shops, a golf course, community campus housing the library, swimming pool and sporting facilities as well as primary and secondary schools. The area is renowned for outdoor activities including white water rafting, abseiling, gorge walking, canyoning, and mountain biking. 

DIRECTIONS
Take the A849 from Aberfeldy for about 9 miles passing through Kenmore and continuing on to Fearnan. At the tall hedge beside the war memorial,  turn right and continue uphill into the village passing the village hall on the left. Take the next left at Dalchiaran and follow road round keeping left, down the single lane road, where you will find Brambles, second on your right.
